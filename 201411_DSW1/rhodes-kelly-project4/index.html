History of Cross-stitch

According to Wikipedia, cross-stich is the oldest form of embroidery in the world.Cross-stitch is simply making patterns or images from X-shaped stitches. After the 2009 recession, many young people became interested in crafting. Cross-stitch that combined tongue-in-cheek sayings with traditional motifs became popular.

Important Dates:

1000 BC Cross-stitch invented
1900 AD Industrial Revolution allows mass consumption of cross-stitch
1989 AD World's largest cross-stitch created

Materials Needed for Cross-stitch

To create your cross-stitch work of art, you simply need fabric, thread and a needle -- all of which can be found at a local craft store. Beginners would find using a pattern helpful.

Fabric

Aida fabric is widely used for cross-stitch projects. Fabrics are categorized by threads per inch (referred to as 'count'), which can range from 11 to 40 count. Aida fabric comes in a variety of colors, but usually only white, linen or black is available at your local craft store; to find the more exotic colored fabric, you will have to look online.

Thread

The thread used in cross-stitch projects is embroidery floss. A wide range of colors is available at your local craft store. Floss even comes in metallic or tied-dyed colors!
